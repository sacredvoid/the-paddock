# Abu Dhabi 2021: The Controversial Finale

December 12, 2021. The Yas Marina Circuit in Abu Dhabi. Lewis Hamilton and Max Verstappen arrived at the final race of the season tied on points, 369.5 each. It was the first time since 1974 that the top two championship contenders entered the last race level. What followed was one of the most dramatic, controversial, and bitterly debated conclusions to a season in the history of any sport.

## The Season of War

The 2021 championship battle between **Lewis Hamilton** and **Max Verstappen** had been building all year. It was a generational clash: Hamilton, the seven-time champion chasing a record eighth title, against Verstappen, the prodigiously talented challenger in his prime. Their rivalry had produced wheel-to-wheel battles at nearly every race, punctuated by collisions at Silverstone and Monza that sent both cars into barriers and both camps into fury.

**Mercedes** and **Red Bull** fought with equal intensity off track. Protests, technical queries, and political maneuvering became weekly occurrences. The atmosphere in the paddock was toxic and thrilling in equal measure.

By Abu Dhabi, the championship was a coin flip. Verstappen led on race wins (9 to Hamilton's 8), meaning he would win the title on countback if both drivers failed to score. Hamilton needed to finish ahead of Verstappen to become champion.

## The Race

Hamilton dominated from the start. He took the lead on the opening lap and steadily pulled away from Verstappen, whose Red Bull could not match the Mercedes' pace on the Yas Marina circuit. By lap 36 of 58, Hamilton's lead was over 11 seconds. The title was slipping away from Verstappen.

Red Bull had already played their strategic cards, pitting Verstappen for fresh tyres in an attempt to undercut Hamilton. It hadn't worked. Hamilton was controlling the race with the calm precision that had defined his career.

## The Latifi Crash

On lap 53, with just five laps remaining, **Nicholas Latifi** crashed his Williams into the barriers at Turn 14. The **safety car** was deployed immediately. Hamilton, still leading, had a decision to make: pit for fresh tyres and risk losing track position, or stay out on old tyres and rely on the safety car eating up the remaining laps.

Mercedes chose to keep Hamilton out. It was the logical call. If the safety car period lasted long enough (as was typical for a crash requiring barrier repair and car removal), the race would end behind the safety car and Hamilton would be champion. Red Bull pitted Verstappen for fresh soft tyres. He had nothing to lose.

## The Decision

What happened next changed the outcome of the 2021 World Championship and triggered a crisis of confidence in F1's race direction.

With the crashed car cleared and lapped cars between Hamilton and Verstappen, **FIA Race Director Michael Masi** faced a choice. Under standard procedure, all lapped cars would be allowed to unlap themselves before the safety car came in, a process that would have taken long enough to end the race under safety car conditions. Alternatively, the lapped cars could remain in place and the race would restart with backmarkers between Hamilton and Verstappen.

Masi chose a third option that was not explicitly outlined in the regulations. He ordered **only the lapped cars between Hamilton and Verstappen** to unlap themselves (five cars), while the remaining lapped cars stayed in position. This cleared the path for a direct Hamilton-Verstappen battle. He then brought the safety car in at the end of lap 57, setting up a final-lap showdown.

## The Last Lap

Hamilton, on 44-lap-old hard tyres, lined up against Verstappen on fresh soft tyres. The outcome was almost inevitable. Verstappen attacked into Turn 5, passing Hamilton on the outside. Hamilton fought back briefly but had no grip to resist. Verstappen held the lead through the remaining corners and crossed the line to win the race and the World Championship.

The Red Bull garage erupted. Verstappen screamed on the radio. His father, Jos, wept. It was a moment of pure, overwhelming emotion.

In the Mercedes garage, there was stunned silence. Hamilton, on the cooldown lap, radioed: **"This has been manipulated, man."** Mercedes team principal **Toto Wolff** had been heard shouting at Masi during the safety car period: **"No, Michael, no, no, Michael. That is so not right."**

## The Fallout

Mercedes protested the result immediately on two grounds: that the safety car procedure had not been correctly followed, and that Verstappen had briefly overtaken Hamilton before the official restart. Both protests were rejected by the stewards.

Mercedes considered appealing to the **International Court of Appeal** but ultimately decided not to, releasing a statement that expressed their disappointment but accepted the result "in the interests of the sport." Hamilton disappeared from public view for weeks. He did not attend the FIA's prize-giving gala.

The controversy dominated global sports coverage. The debate was not about whether Verstappen was a worthy champion (his season had been extraordinary) but about whether the process that determined the final result was fair, consistent, and in accordance with the rules.

## The FIA Investigation

The FIA commissioned an investigation into the events of Abu Dhabi. The resulting report, published ahead of the 2022 season, found that Masi had acted "in good faith" but acknowledged that the application of the safety car rules was not consistent with established procedure.

The consequences were significant:

- **Michael Masi was removed** as FIA Race Director.
- A new system of **two rotating Race Directors** was introduced, supported by a dedicated team in a remote operations room.
- The safety car restart procedures were clarified to prevent a similar situation from recurring.
- A new protocol was established requiring all lapped cars to unlap themselves, or none.

## Legacy

Abu Dhabi 2021 remains the most debated moment in modern F1. For Verstappen fans, it was a thrilling finale to a season where their driver had been the best on the grid. For Hamilton supporters, it was a championship stolen by a procedural error. For neutrals, it was a reminder that human decisions at critical moments can override hundreds of laps of racing.

Hamilton returned for the 2022 season and continued competing, but the eighth world championship record remained out of reach. Verstappen went on to win the 2022, 2023, and 2024 titles, establishing himself as one of the greatest drivers of all time. But the shadow of Abu Dhabi lingered over all of it, an asterisk that both fans and the FIA wished they could erase.

The night race in the desert was supposed to be a coronation. Instead, it became a reckoning, for the FIA, for the sport's credibility, and for the idea that the rules apply equally to everyone at all times.
